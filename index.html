§ 01 — Snapshot

A $192 billion
visitor economy

In the 12 months to December 2025, Australians and international visitors combined to spend $192.4 billion — a 7% increase year-on-year. Domestic day trips surged 19% in spend while international visitor expenditure jumped 19%, signalling a robust post-pandemic rebound across all traveller segments.

§ 03 — International Visitors

The world
discovers Australia

8.9 million international visitors arrived in 2025, spending $39.2 billion. New Zealand remained our closest travel partner, while China rebounded strongly with 974,000 visitors — a 17% increase — and the UK saw a 19% surge.

Top Source Countries
Trips ('000) · Year ending December 2025

New Zealand, China, and the UK are Australia's top three visitor markets by trip volume.

Visitor Spend in Australia
Spend in Australia ($M) · Year ending December 2025

Chinese visitors generate far more spend per trip than any other nationality — $11.2bn from just 974K trips.

Purpose of International Visits — Trips vs Spend

Holiday visitors numbered 3.6M but education visitors — though only 540K — contributed $14.5bn in spend due to longer stays. This reflects the enormous economic value of Australia's international education sector.

International Visitor Spend by State

§ 05 — The Spending Story

How travellers
spend their dollars

Beyond trip counts, the real economic story is spending behaviour. Day trippers spent 19% more year-on-year; international visitors 19% more. Yet domestic overnight spend fell 2%, revealing a subtle shift in how Australians travel at home.

Trips vs Spend — Traveller Segment Comparison
Year ending December 2025 · Bubble size = total nights spent

International visitors take far fewer trips than domestic travellers but stay much longer (avg 37 nights vs 3.4 nights per overnight trip), generating disproportionately high economic impact per traveller.

"Tourism Research Australia forecasts total tourism spend to reach $233.4 billion by 2030 — sustained by international recovery, growing Asia-Pacific travel, and the enduring appeal of Australia's natural wonders."

Source: TRA Tourism Forecasts for Australia, 2025
